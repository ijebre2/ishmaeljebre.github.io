Final Year Undergraduate Thesis Project- Instantons and Its Applications

My undergraduate thesis, "Instantons and Its Applications," conducted at Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology, delved into the fascinating world of instantons‚Äîpseudo-particles and solutions to non-linear wave equations in theoretical and mathematical physics. Under the supervision of Dr. Ishmael Takyi, this research aimed to explore the interactions of instantons and anti-instantons, specifically utilizing the œï4 model.

    Key Components

  • The methodology involved a rigorous derivation of the Lagrangian and Hamiltonian field theories, including the application of Noether's theorem to understand conservation laws. A core component of the study was the derivation of instanton and anti-instanton solutions, along with their energy densities, from the œï4 theory.

  • Through graphical analysis and illustrative examples using MATLAB application, the thesis demonstrated key findings regarding the dynamic behavior of these pseudo-particles. It was observed that at initial stages, instantons and anti-instantons tend to move apart, resulting in energy loss. Conversely, at later times, they exhibit a tendency to approach each other, leading to a regain of energy upon collision. Furthermore, the research highlighted that an increase in velocity correlates with a wider separation distance between instantons and anti-instantons.

  • his project significantly enhanced my understanding of advanced mathematical physics, non-linear dynamics, and the application of theoretical concepts to model complex physical phenomena, reinforcing my analytical and problem-solving skills.
