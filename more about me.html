About Me

I am Ishmael Jebre, a highly passionate and accomplished Mathematics graduate with a Master of Science from the University of Delhi (ICCR Scholar) and a Bachelor of Science from Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology. My academic journey has provided me with a robust foundation in theoretical and applied mathematics, rigorous problem- solving, and foundational data science principles, including areas like Support Vector Machines and Optimization.

My professional experience spans diverse and impactful roles. As a Liaison Officer at the High Commission of Ghana to India, I honed my diplomatic, cross-cultural communication, and logistical coordination skills, contributing to international relations and supporting high- level delegations. My significant experience as a Teaching Assistant at KNUST and a Mathematics Instructor at Sahrich Academy underscores my ability to simplify complex mathematical concepts, mentor students, and foster academic growth in STEM fields.

With a strong interest in leveraging quantitative insights for real-world impact, my future endeavors are focused on mathematical modeling for critical areas such as food security in Ghana and epidemiological challenges in public health. I am proficient in Python (with foundational data science tools like Pandas and NumPy), MATLAB, and LaTeX, complemented by strong soft skills in communication, leadership, and cross-cultural collaboration.

I am a global scholar with a commitment to continuous learning and community engagement, I am driven to apply analytical thinking and machine learning to address pressing societal problems, always seeking opportunities to contribute meaningfully through data-driven solutions.
